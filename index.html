Effective Cross-Lingual Many-to-Many Voice Conversion toward Natural Pronunciation Transfer

Chan-Ho Song, Hyun-Wook Yoon, Jin-Seob Kim, Ryuichi Yamamoto, Ryo Terashima, Eunwoo Song

Abstract

Recent advances in speech self-supervised learning (SSL) models have enabled voice conversion (VC) systems to effectively disentangle linguistic content from speaker identity. However, cross-lingual models still struggle with unnatural in- tonation due to difficulties in separating language-specific pro- nunciation from speaker timbre. To address this challenge, we proposes X2M-VC, a cross-lingual, many-to-many VC model that effectively converts the speaker timbre across different languages while preserving the natural pronunciation. Through a comprehensive study of SSL models, we identify the most effective representations for capturing both linguistic content and language-specific attribute from the given speech. By incorporat- ing these SSL-based content representations with explicit speaker and language embeddings, our X2M-VC model ensures robust transfer of speaker characteristics while maintaining the orig- inal language’s native pronunciation. Objective and subjective evaluations on a multilingual corpus including English, Korean, and Japanese demonstrate significant improvements in voice conversion quality and accuracy across multiple languages.

In this paper, we incorporated the VAE structure into the learning process and confirmed that it is possible to distinguish between the characteristics of real (recorded) audio and augmented (voice converted) audio. By using only the characteristics of real audio for inference, we were able to synthesize higher quality audio than simply using an augmented database. The following represents the embeddings in the latent space of the learned VAE, differentiated by each characteristic. Each point signifies a posterior embedding extracted from the audio, and is visualized with different colors or shapes based on aspects like language, speaker, or the presence of voice conversion (VC). As seen in the figure, it was evident that the boundaries were more distinctly defined from the perspective of VC presence compared to other characteristics.
